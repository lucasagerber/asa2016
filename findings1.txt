Reviewers generally review authors of the same gender. That publications--across 8 of the 9 sampled--exhibit the same trend implies that, at the site of literary consecration, gender is salient as a determinant of who reviews whom.  Supposing that female reviewers utilize different evaluative practices than male reviewers, the matching effect would then manifest as differences in the evaluations of male and female poets in the field as a whole.
  
In short, who reviews whom matters.
